Harvey's Kimchi

Your journey to tasty, funky, homemade kimchi starts here!

What You'll Need

Ingredients

Mandatory

  • Cabbage (Napa / Chinese Leaf)
  • Gochujang Paste
  • Garlic
  • Soy Sauce

Optional (but tasty!)

  • Carrot
  • Spring Onion
  • Ginger
  • Brown Sugar

Equipment

Mandatory

  • Large Mixing Bowl
  • Fermentation Jar (or any tall, clean, food-safe container with a lid)

Optional

  • Glass Fermentation Weight

The Recipe

Part 1: Soak the Cabbage (~6 hours)

  1. Wash and chop cabbage into bite-sized pieces (around 1-2 inches). Make sure a few larger leaves are wide enough to cover the opening of your jar - we'll need them later!
  2. Place cabbage in your large mixing bowl. For every 100g of cabbage, add 2g of salt and mix well to distribute.
    A typical supermarket cabbage is about 600g, so you'd use 12g of salt.
  3. Add enough water to completely submerge the cabbage.
  4. Sprinkle a few extra grams of salt on top.
  5. Use a small plate to weigh the cabbage down, keeping it all underwater.
  6. Cover the bowl and let it soak for 4-8 hours, or overnight.
    I use a large pan lid to cover my bowl. Anything works!

Part 2: Mix the Magic (~20 mins)

  1. Scoop out ~200ml (about a cup) of the salty cabbage water and set it aside. Drain the rest, but don't squeeze the cabbage dry.
  2. Peel and chop your garlic (and ginger, if using). Slice or peel any other veg into thin strips.
  3. Add the garlic, ginger, and other veg to the cabbage.
  4. Time for the sauce! Add 1 big tablespoon of Gochujang per 200g of cabbage (e.g., 3 tbsp for a 600g cabbage). Use more if you want it spicier!
    I use an ice cream scoop instead of a tablespoon. All my normal spoons kept bending when mixing and packing down the kimchi...
  5. Add 1 tablespoon of Soy Sauce per 300g of cabbage (2 tbsp for 600g). Add a teaspoon of brown sugar if you have it.
  6. Experiment with other seasonings! Go wild!
    Pastes work better than powders to avoid a gritty texture. I'm currently enjoying adding a spoonful of fermented soy bean paste.
  7. Mix everything thoroughly until the cabbage is evenly coated.
  8. Transfer the kimchi to your fermentation jar, leaving most of the sauce behind for now. Pack it down tightly to remove air gaps, leaving about 3-5cm of space at the top. Place your largest cabbage leaves on top.
  9. If you have a fermentation weight, place it on top of the big leaves.
    No weight? No problem. A large cabbage leaf works fine. Or get creative! I've used the plastic insert from a jar of peppers, and even a small freezer bag filled with a little water. Just make sure it's clean and food-safe.
  10. Press down on the top layer. The juice should rise up and cover the cabbage. If not, add the leftover sauce from the bowl, and then the reserved cabbage water if you still need more liquid. Everything must be submerged!

Part 3: Let it Ferment (3+ days)

  1. Seal your jar and place it in a cool, dark spot. Put a small tray or plate underneath to catch any potential bubbly overflow.
  2. Let it ferment for 3 to 5 days. If your jar doesn't self-vent, "burp" it once a day by briefly loosening the lid to release pressure.
    Even with a self-venting jar, I like to check on it every day to see the bubbles!
  3. After 3 days, it'll be tangy and fresh. My sweet spot is 5 days. The longer you wait, the funkier and more flavourful it gets.
  4. Going past 7 days increases the risk of spoilage, so only do this if you're confident everything was sterilised and kept fully submerged.

Part 4: Storage

  1. Once it's fermented to your liking, move the jar to the fridge. This will mostly pause the fermentation process.
  2. It will keep in the fridge for up to 2 months, and likely even longer if you did a good job keeping things sterile!

Enjoy your homemade kimchi!
