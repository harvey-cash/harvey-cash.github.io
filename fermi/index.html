‚õπÔ∏è Garden Games

Getting Fermi to use his nose in the garden is a great way to tire him out both mentally and physically. Mix and match these activities to keep him engaged and working!


Scatter Feeding

Take some kibble (or treats) and toss them into a patch of long grass, while saying "Search!"


Fetching a Ball

Roll a ball and say "Fetch!". When he brings it back, offer a treat in exchange for the ball ("Give").


Tug of War (also Indoors)

Hold up a toy and say "Tug!". Let him pull on it for a while, then either: 1. let him win, or 2. say "Give!" and wait for him to release it. Then reward with a treat!


üè† Crate & Separation Anxiety

For the first few days of Fermi's stay with you, you do not need to close him in his crate. Simply set it up somewhere accessible as a familiar refuge for him to use if he wants to.

Later on, once he is calm and relaxed, you can send him to his crate for his regular nap time.

Crate Command: "Go to bed"

Please put his panda blanket and a toy or yak cheese in with him, then cover the crate to make it cozy.

Fermi has severe separation anxiety and cannot be left completely alone at home.

If he gets very stressed (howling/barking) in his crate, please let him out calmly. The goal is to avoid negative associations. Don't reward the behaviour with treats or fuss, just calmly open the door.

üéæ Commands & Play

Fermi is very treat-motivated! Training games are a great way to bond and tire him out indoors.

Indoor Game: "Find It!"

  1. Show him a toy, then have him "Stay".
  2. Hide it in another room.
  3. Return and say "Find It!" with open hands.
  4. When he brings it back, say "Give" and offer your hand.
  5. Reward him when he drops it in your hand. Repeat!

üêæ Walks & Anxiety Management

For the first few days with you, you do not need to take Fermi out on walks. Play time indoors and in the garden will be enough, while he is settling in.

Later on, or if you need to take him out in an emergency, please keep the following guidance in mind:

Walk Essentials

  • Gear: Always wear both harness and collar.
  • Calm & Confident: If he's calm, clip lead to his collar. Say "heel" and treat for staying by your side.
  • Anxious & Pulling: If he's anxious, clip lead to his harness to protect his neck.
  • Freedom: He can't go off-leash near others. Use his long-line for some running freedom in safe, empty areas.

Managing Reactivity (Dogs)

  • Avoid if possible: Your main goal is a peaceful walk. Crossing the street is a success!
  • See a dog far away? Treat generously! Show him dogs from a distance are a good thing.
  • Dog gets too close?
    1. Stay calm, don't pull the lead.
    2. Use high-value treats to lure him away.
    3. Use the cue: "Okay, let's go!"
    4. Praise and treat him once you're clear.
Most Important Rule: You **do not** need to walk him if it is stressful for either of you. Short toilet breaks are fine. A happy dog is better than a stressed dog.
